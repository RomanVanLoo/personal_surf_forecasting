The Big Picture

Small ENE swell (0.9-1.2m, short period 7s) continues through the week. Dawn patrol remains the only worthwhile window — winds are lightest before 8am across all regions. NE onshores build every afternoon. Byron and Noosa are tiny (knee-thigh) and not worth the drive. The real story: a monster swell is building for Tue 24 March (2.4-3.7m at D-Bah, 3-4.5m at Lennox Wed 25). That's the event of the trip. Thu 26 is the sweet spot — swell easing to 1.5-2.4m with near-calm dawn winds.

Best advice for Wednesday

Scout spots for when the big swell hits next week.

Last day of the small stuff. Same dawn-is-the-window pattern. Consider a Ballina road trip — Sharpes Beach and Shelly Beach are gorgeous, empty, and Cato-friendly. Saturday the swell starts shifting and by Sunday there's more shape. Monday onwards is when it gets serious.

Days to Watch

Sunday 22 March
D-Bah: 0.9-1.2m  |  Lennox: 0.9-1.2m
More shape and push to the surf. New longer-period swell (10-11s) arriving from E/SE. Cleanest AM. First hint of the building swell. Worth an early session.
Monday 23 March
D-Bah: 0.9-2.0m  |  Lennox: 0.9-1.8m
Building E swell through the day. Possible southerlies as well. Size ramps from 0.9m dawn to 2.0m by evening at D-Bah. Could see first overhead sets late in the day. Strong winds though (23-35kph). Morning before it gets wild.
Tuesday 24 March
D-Bah: 2.4-3.7m  |  Lennox: 2.4-3.0m  |  The Pass: 1.2-2.4m
The swell event of the trip. Solid overhead-to-double overhead. Every exposed break firing. Strong winds 17-43kph — messy but powerful. Too big and rough for Cato. Roman: hunt for the cleanest window. Sheltered spots like Currumbin might be the play for Cato with chest-high filtered swell.
Wednesday 25 March
D-Bah: 2.4-3.7m  |  Lennox: 3.0-4.5m  |  The Pass: 1.8-2.4m
Peak of the swell. Lennox could be absolutely firing — 3-4.5m. Sunshine Coast says possible AM offshores. If winds cooperate this could be epic. Even Byron/The Pass finally has proper surf (1.8-2.4m).
Thursday 26 March — THE SWEET SPOT
D-Bah: 1.5-2.4m  |  Lennox: 1.8-2.4m  |  The Pass: 1.2-1.8m
Swell easing to very surfable range. D-Bah dawn: 4-5kph wind — near glassy! This could be the best day of the trip. Shoulder-to-overhead clean surf. Cato could join at sheltered spots if it's on the lower end. Points like Lennox and Snapper will be world-class if the wind holds.
Friday-Saturday 27-28 March
D-Bah: 1.2-2.0m  |  Lennox: 1.5-2.4m
Easing further but still good quality surf. Lennox holding size better than GC. Fun overhead sessions possible. Keep watching wind direction.
